About

Built by people who understand emerging-market operations.

DairyCare is a product of Silicate Labs — an enterprise software company headquartered in Islamabad, Pakistan, building the infrastructure that organizations doing consequential work actually need.

Silicate Labs

Software built for this context — not adapted from someone else's.

Silicate Labs is an enterprise software company headquartered in Islamabad, Pakistan. We build intelligence platforms, payment infrastructure, logistics systems, AI tools, and communication products — a growing ecosystem of solutions designed for organizations that cannot afford to operate on second-rate software.

We started with a simple observation: the organizations doing the most consequential work in emerging markets were running on tools built for someone else's context. We set out to change that — not by adapting foreign products, but by building purpose-built software from the ground up, starting with Pakistan and expanding to every market that shares the same gap.

Today, Silicate Labs ships products across six industry verticals — financial intelligence, logistics, industrial data, commerce, workforce, and media. DairyCare is our answer for agriculture: dairy farms generating real operational data every day, with no system built to make that data useful. Each product begins with a specific problem no existing tool was solving well enough. That discipline is what keeps our ecosystem coherent rather than scattered.

We are based in Islamabad. Our infrastructure is here. Our team is here. And our conviction is that serious enterprise software can come from anywhere — including here.

Founder's Note

Why we're building this.

FOUNDER
PHOTO
"Building Silicate Labs was never about building a company. It was about solving problems that were genuinely unsolved — in markets that had been told to wait for solutions built elsewhere."

I started with the problems I could see clearly: Pakistani freelancers who couldn't get paid internationally. Agencies running their entire operation across five disconnected tools. Factories generating enormous amounts of operational data with no way to make it useful. Investors and founders with no reliable way to find each other. Dairy farms tracking herds of hundreds of animals on paper registers that no one could reconcile at month-end.

Each product in our ecosystem started as an answer to one of those specific questions. What held them together was a single principle: if something is worth building, it is worth building well — with no shortcuts, no placeholders, and no assumption that the user will accept less because of where they are.

The name Silicate Labs reflects that conviction. Silicon — the material that computes — refined, structured, and made useful. That is what we do with information, with systems, and with the problems our clients bring us.

We are still early. There are more products on the roadmap, more markets to serve, and more problems worth solving. If you are building something serious and need infrastructure that takes your work as seriously as you do — we should talk.

— Founder, Silicate Labs
